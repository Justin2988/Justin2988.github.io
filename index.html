Profile

A scholar-practitioner orientation.

I work at the intersection of higher education administration, instructional design, and adult learning. For more than ten years I have developed and facilitated training that builds measurable knowledge, skills, and performance outcomes, first designing and delivering it in the Michigan National Guard, then leading enterprise technology programs, and now supporting one of the largest health system modernizations in the United States.

That combination is deliberate. Grounded theory research, curriculum development, and learner assessment on one side; program leadership, Agile delivery, and data-informed decision-making on the other. My doctoral research examines the same tension I have spent my career managing: the distance between what a program teaches and what its graduates actually do.

I am completing an EdD in Educational Leadership, hold a Certified ScrumMaster credential, and maintain an active federal Secret security clearance.

03 — Research

Doctoral research

Instructional Balance in Community College Health Sciences Programs A Qualitative Classical Grounded Theory Study

Community college Health Sciences programs prepare learners for professional entry through a combination of theoretical and practical instruction. Variations in that balance have been documented — but the social processes students and faculty use to navigate them had not been explained.

Working from anonymous open-ended surveys with 18 students and faculty across the Great Lakes region, constant comparative analysis surfaced a shared main concern: the gap between classroom instruction and clinical practice.
