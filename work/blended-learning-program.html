Battalion Blended Learning Program

Where I learned to teach adults: designing and delivering blended instruction for battalion personnel, on subjects where getting the learning wrong had real consequences.

Role

Human Resources Specialist · Team Leader

Organization

Michigan National Guard — 126th Infantry Regiment

Period

October 2012 — October 2018

Reach

Battalion level · 150+ personnel supported

01 Context

Battalion personnel are a demanding instructional audience in the most useful way. They are adults with real expertise, competing obligations outside the unit, wide variation in prior exposure to the material, and drill time that is finite and contested. Nobody is there to be talked at.

The subject matter ranged from administrative processes people needed in order to be paid and promoted correctly, to organizational development and mandatory curricula on diversity and harassment awareness — content where a session that produces compliance without comprehension has accomplished nothing.

02 My role

As a Human Resources Specialist I designed and delivered the learning solutions supporting battalion personnel, and was promoted to team leader overseeing 5+ soldiers. Alongside instruction I managed the full HR lifecycle — onboarding, benefits, payroll, promotions, retirements, and separations — for 150+ personnel.

03 Approach

  • Blend by content type, not by convenience. Combined classroom instruction with digital training, assigning each mode the material it actually suited — procedural knowledge asynchronously, judgment and socially weighted content in the room.
  • Design toward required outcomes. Applied instructional design and adult-learning principles to build the knowledge, skills, and behavioral outcomes the unit's objectives required — starting from the performance, not the slide deck.
  • Facilitate sensitive curricula seriously. Delivered diversity and harassment-awareness training at battalion level, where facilitation quality determines whether the session changes anything or hardens resistance.
  • Treat service delivery as instruction too. Ran the HR lifecycle with an emphasis on responsive customer service, which in practice meant explaining systems clearly enough that people could navigate them independently next time.

04 Outcome

  • Blended learning solutions delivered across battalion personnel, building the knowledge, skills, and behavioral outcomes required for unit objectives.
  • Organizational development and mandatory training programs facilitated at battalion level.
  • Promotion to team leader over 5+ soldiers.
  • Full HR lifecycle managed for 150+ personnel.

An audience that will tell you immediately when you are wasting their time is the best instructional design training available.

05 Why this matters in higher education

Adult and non-traditional learners are the majority population at many institutions, and they behave exactly like the audience described above: experienced, time-poor, instrumentally motivated, and quick to disengage from instruction that does not respect those conditions.

The facilitation experience carries over as directly as the design experience. Required training on difficult subjects is a permanent feature of institutional life, and the difference between a session that lands and one that breeds cynicism is almost entirely in how it is designed and led.
