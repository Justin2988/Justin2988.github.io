Executive Decision-Support Dashboards

Turning distributed operational data into a single view that program leadership could actually act on — the institutional-research problem, posed in a federal health IT setting.

Role

Support Analyst · Consultant, VA OIT Support

Organization

Take2 Consulting, LLC

Period

January 2022 — Present

Stack

Power BI · Power Query · DAX · Power Platform

01 Context

Enterprise IT operations for a program spanning 170+ facilities produce operational data faster than any leadership team can absorb it, and from more sources than any one person tracks. Deployment status lives in one place, incidents in another, events in a third. Each is accurate. None of them, alone, answers the question a decision-maker is actually asking.

The result is a familiar failure mode: reporting that is technically complete and practically useless. Leadership either waits for a human to assemble the picture manually — which is slow and does not scale — or makes the call on partial information.

02 My role

I design and maintain the executive-level Power BI dashboards for the program, including the Deployment Support Operations (DSO) and Event dashboards. The work spans the full path: understanding what decision a stakeholder is trying to make, modeling the data to support it, and building the visualization that makes the answer obvious.

03 Approach

  • Start from the decision, not the data. A dashboard earns its place only if someone changes what they do because of it. Every measure traces back to a question a stakeholder has to answer.
  • Model before you visualize. Built the transformation and modeling layer in Power Query and DAX so that the data arriving at the visual is already trustworthy — most dashboard failures are modeling failures wearing a chart.
  • Design for a mixed audience. The same dashboard serves program leadership and the cross-functional teams doing the work. Those audiences need different altitudes of the same truth, without maintaining two versions of it.
  • Connect reporting to root cause. Paired the dashboards with root cause analysis on recurring issues, so the reporting surfaced systemic patterns instead of just counting incidents.
  • Automate the routine. Applied Power Platform low-code automation and generative AI tooling to prototype reporting workflows and cut manual effort, improving turnaround on stakeholder deliverables.

04 Outcome

  • Complex OIT operational data translated into clear, actionable insight for cross-functional stakeholders and program leadership.
  • Systemic patterns identified across recurring issues, enabling preventive measures rather than repeated remediation.
  • Faster turnaround and higher quality on stakeholder deliverables through automation of routine analytical work.
  • Reporting workflows standardized and documented, so the capability survived team transitions.

05 Why this matters in higher education

This is the core work of institutional research and academic assessment. Enrollment trends, retention and completion, program-level learning outcomes, accreditation evidence, resource utilization — all of it is distributed across systems that were never designed to talk to each other, and all of it has to reach a dean, provost, or board in a form that supports a decision.

My research reinforces the caution I bring to it: metrics are an input to judgment, not a replacement for it. An assessment regime that produces clean numbers nobody believes has failed, no matter how complete the dataset.

The measure of a reporting system is not how much it shows. It is how often it changes what someone decides.
